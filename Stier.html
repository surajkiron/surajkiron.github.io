The Thermal Systems Lab IISc

A summary on what I'm working on at the Interdiscplinary Center for Energy Research (ICER)

Suraj K Nair

The Thermal Systems Lab at ICER is now primarily focused on carbon dioxide(Co2) cycles. Brayton cycles using supercritical CO2 as a working fluid offers the potential for higher thermodynamic cycle efficiencies than steam-based Rankine cycles. Hence, they are of significant interest for power generation applications. sCo2 cycles are used for waste heat recovery and power generation, whereas Transcritical Co2 Cycles are suitable for refrigeration. All such cycles require heat exchangers in the form of recuperators and gas coolers. I'm currently involved in the design and analysis of printed circuit heat exchangers(PCHEs).

We use a thermal resistance network (TRN) model (developed using Python) for full-scale modelling of a PCHE. The geometrical information obtained from the TRN model is used to optimize the overall footprint of the heat exchanger. The Python program is coupled with the CoolProp property database to retrieve the thermodynamic properties across all the nodes. The TRN method is preferred over traditional CFD simulations because it is faster and provides similar accuracies. CFD simulations are useful for determining pressure drop correlations. I use Ansys Fluent for such CFD simulations.
